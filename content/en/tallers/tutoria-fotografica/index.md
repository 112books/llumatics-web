## A different format

Not everyone learns the same way. Some people learn better in a group; others need a space where they don't have to follow anyone else's pace. The tutorial is for the latter — and for the former, when they want to dig into something specific.

It might be to resolve a doubt that's been sitting unanswered for a while. To learn to develop film for the first time without the pressure of a group. To work on a personal photographic project with someone who understands it. To review a series and find out where to improve.

It starts with a brief conversation — by email or phone — to understand where you're coming from and what the session should achieve.

## Examples of possible content

- First time in the darkroom: film development step by step
- Review and editing of a personal photographic project
- Learning to make paper prints from your own negatives
- Understanding why your photos don't come out the way you expect
- Exploring a specific technique: large format, pinhole, alternative processes
- Preparation for a commission or an exhibition

## How it works

Get in touch and briefly explain what you'd like to work on. We agree on the length and content of the session and propose a date. Minimum 2 hours, maximum 4 hours per session.

## What's included

- 1:1 session with Joan (2 to 4 hours, to be agreed)
- Standard lab materials if the session includes hands-on practice (chemicals, paper, film)

## What to bring

- Everything you want to work on: camera, negatives, prints, project, questions. The session is designed from a prior conversation.

## Not included

- Specific non-standard materials (discussed and priced in advance)
- Additional 35mm rolls (~€5/roll)
