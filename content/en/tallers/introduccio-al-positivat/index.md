## The first time under the red light

Walking into a dark lab with the red light on and watching an image slowly appear in the developer tray is an experience you don't forget. No app comes close.

This session is for anyone who has never done it. We work at the enlarger: we project the negative onto the paper, expose, develop and see the result. Simple, but far more satisfying than it sounds.

If you want to go further — local controls, dodging and burning, fibre-base papers — the Darkroom Printing workshop is the natural next step.

## Contents

- Orientation in the darkroom: materials, safety and workflow
- The enlarger: parts, focus and light measurement
- Test strip: how to read it and decide the exposure
- Tray development: developer, stop bath and fixer
- Washing and drying the print
- Reading the result and basic adjustments

## What's included

- RC photographic paper (2–3 sheets)
- Chemicals: paper developer, stop bath, fixer
- Use of the enlarger and darkroom

## What to bring

- Developed 35mm negatives if you have them. If not, we work with sample negatives.
