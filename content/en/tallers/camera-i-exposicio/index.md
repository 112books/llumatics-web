## Where to start

Many people have been taking photos for years without fully understanding why one shot comes out well exposed and another, taken in the same spot, doesn't. The answer is always the same: light, time, and sensitivity. Three variables. Nothing more.

In this workshop we work on exactly that: the relationship between aperture, shutter speed, and ISO, and how to control them to get the exposure you want. We do it with analogue cameras because they force you to think. There's no screen to confirm it and no app to fix it.

## Key contents

- The camera as a light box: parts and how it works
- Aperture: opening, depth of field and bokeh
- Shutter speed: time, movement and stability
- ISO: sensitivity, grain and trade-offs
- The exposure triangle: the relationship between the three variables
- Reading light: the light meter and your eye
- Practice: shooting with an analogue camera in manual mode
