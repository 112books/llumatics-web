## A different kind of camera

The Hasselblad 500 isn't a camera you carry everywhere. It's a camera you use when you want to make a real photograph. The square 6×6cm format, the waist-level finder, the sound of the mirror, the interchangeable film back, the sharpness of Carl Zeiss lenses — everything invites you to slow down, look, and decide.

In this workshop there's time for everything: to understand the system, to shoot with intention and to see the developed results the same day. We finish with a little something to eat.

## Key contents

- The Hasselblad 500 system: body, dual shutter releases and synchronised operation
- The interchangeable back: loading a 120 roll and mid-roll swaps
- The waist-level finder: composition and light reading in square format
- Lenses: available focal lengths and their uses
- Double exposures: how to activate them and when to use them
- Remote shutter and cable: practical possibilities
- Tips and system quirks that don't appear in the manuals
- Practice: a full 120 roll (12 frames 6×6)
- Roll development and digitisation of all frames
