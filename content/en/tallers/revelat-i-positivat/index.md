## Everything in one day

The complete analogue process has two moments: film development and darkroom printing. They're usually learned separately, in two workshops on two different dates. In this format we combine them: you develop in the morning, you print in the afternoon.

No prior experience needed. It's a great entry point for anyone who wants to understand the full process in one go, without waiting between sessions.

## How the day is structured

**Morning (10:00–14:00) — Film development**
- Preparing the chemicals
- Loading the spiral in total darkness
- Step-by-step development: developer, stop bath, fixer, wash
- Inspecting and drying the negative

*Break — 2 hours free*

**Afternoon (16:00–20:00) — Darkroom printing**
- The enlarger: how it works and focusing
- Test strip and reading the exposure
- Print development in the tray
- Basic controls: contrast and zone adjustments
- Finishing and presentation

## What's included

- One 35mm B&W roll
- All chemicals: film developer, stop bath, fixer (morning session)
- Printing chemicals: paper developer, stop bath, fixer (afternoon session)
- RC photographic paper (2–3 prints)
- Full use of the darkroom all day

## What to bring

- Nothing. If you have additional exposed rolls you'd like to develop, bring them along.

## Not included

- Additional 35mm B&W rolls (+€5/roll)
- Extra paper for additional prints (+€2/sheet RC, +€4/sheet fibre)
