We ran the first film developing workshop. Not the first I've ever done, but the first here, in the new space.

The mechanics are the same as always: mix the chemicals, load the reel in total darkness, develop with care and wash with patience. But the reaction when someone opens the tank and sees the negative for the first time — that look of surprise mixed with "of course, it was always going to work" — never gets old.

One participant did the workshop with her wedding roll, which had been sitting undeveloped in a drawer for 14 years. We didn't know if there would be anything on it. There was. Thirty-six frames from 2012, perfectly exposed, perfect.

That's something no app can give you.
