The day started at 10:00 in the morning, with Nau Bostik open to everyone.

At La Perversa we held an open day: the darkroom accessible, the studio available, and if you brought your camera and photographic paper, you could develop your own photos from the day. No fuss. Those who wanted to learn, learnt. Those who wanted to watch, watched.

The experimental pinhole workshop was led by **Alfonso de Castro** with **Santi Pladellorens** from Analogueworks, who brought along some of his cameras in very different formats for participants to use. Fifteen euros, limited places, and one of the best sessions of the year.

I was running the photocall: pinhole portraits. Each portrait, unique. Each exposure, a decision.

**Tiempo Y Luz** with their photominute cameras rounded out the scene: immediate, intimate, beautiful pictures.

The Perverse Market, exhibitions opening throughout the morning, a camera obscura set up for visitors, and pinhole-camera ballot boxes for voting — because it was an important political day and we thought it was better to do it in style.

And the Lenin. A large Lenin head with a pinhole camera built in, wandering around the Nau taking photos. It didn't really need explaining, and it didn't have an explanation anyway.

It was a very good day.
